Church

The Silliman University Church traces its origins to the arrival of American Presbyterian missionaries in the Philippines in 1899, with the founding of Silliman Institute in 1901. Initially, services were held alongside the school’s first classes, and by 1911, the Presbyterian Church of Dumaguete was officially established, later becoming the Dumaguete Evangelical Church. Due to differing needs and languages between the town and school congregations, the two separated in 1916, forming the Silliman Student Church, which evolved into the Silliman University Church. Focused on ministering to students, faculty, and alumni of diverse faiths, the church emphasizes evangelistic witness, spiritual growth, and translating faith into action while respecting individual religious practices.

Galilean Fellowship

On November 19, 2024 we attended the Galilean Fellowship during the UCLEM. The event was held to foster camaraderie and togetherness through discussing our problems which they called life’s giants. At the beginning they told each one of us to reach into a paper bag they had prepared up front. Each one of us was skeptical not knowing what was in the bag so we introduced ourselves, reached into the bag, and went back to our seats. What was important wasn’t what was in the bag but rather our bravery for reaching into it despite not knowing anything. Through this activity we learned that despite life’s giants we must be like David the shepherd boy who didn’t falter in the face of Goliath but faced him head on with his faith in God. The speaker really left an impression on me as he told his story about being a teacher in his last year of college and he left it all to follow in the footsteps of the Lord. This activity allowed me to really get to know and strengthen my faith together with others through fun activities. Because of this activity, I enhanced my faith in the Lord in order to conquer life’s giants.
